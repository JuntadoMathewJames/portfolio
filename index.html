About Me


Hello, I am James Mathew Juntado and I am currently a fourth-year student in Ateneo de Naga University taking up Bachelor of Science in Information Technology. I started programming back when I was in Senior high school in Camarines Sur National High School. Back then, I was really a newbie who didn't know anything about programming and just thought that programming is all about gaming and stuff but apparently, programming was more than gaming.

My first ever programming language that my teacher taught me was Java, I remembered how happy I was to be able to instruct a computer to print “Hello World” to the point that I even thought that I was already a very good programmer being able to print that phrase. It was all fun and easy until our teacher started discussing intermediate programming functions like the loops, arrays, classes, objects, etc… At first I really had a hard time understanding those concepts but as time went by I just realized that it is not really necessary for me to memorize and focus on the syntax but rather it is more important for me to be able to understand programming at its core. Because I realized that syntax changes for each programming language but the fundamentals behind it are always the same. Realizing these things, I was able to understand more about programming and sooner began to love it. Especially when our teacher started discussing web development using the usual php, javascript, html, and css I became more intrigued with it. And at that point, I decided that I want to be a web developer that can develop life changing applications. So I graduated in senior high school and then college came. My first two years in college were really easy for me because the majority of the lessons are just like a recap of what my senior high school teacher taught me. But then third year high school came and I was introduced to concepts I am not really familiar with like machine learning, quality management, networking etc… It was really hard at first but given my versatility I was able to adjust and understand the concepts of these lessons.

Right now, I am able to develop a working web application by myself but I am honestly not very good at front-end development because I excel more in back-end development. Some of my work are web-based point of sales that I implemented for our tailoring business, and my capstone project which is E-bakwit, an evacuation management system for CSWDO Naga. At the end, I am really having fun developing applications and it is such an honor for me to see people using the applications I developed.
